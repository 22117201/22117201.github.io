Video games development: 'Wales can be global player'

"Everything is here" for Wales to be a global player in the video games industry, according to a man training the next generation of developers. Lecturer Simon Reed is one of a number of industry figures pushing for Wales to tap into the huge global market. With 30 games companies, Cardiff lags far behind other UK towns and cities, including Bristol which has 50. But there are 65 studios across Wales and some founders think they no longer need to be based in big cities. The global gaming industry is worth more than £110bn and is projected to grow to £144bn by 2021, according to industry body UK Interactive Entertainment (UKIE).
